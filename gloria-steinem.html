Gloria Steinem (Journalist) - On This Day

Profession: Journalist

Biography: Gloria Steinem is an American journalist and activist who became nationally recognized as a leader and a spokeswoman for the American feminist movement in the late 1960s and early 1970s. Steinem was a prominent figure in the fight for women's rights and played a pivotal role in shaping feminist discourse and activism in the United States.

Steinem began her career in journalism in New York City in the early 1960s. She first gained attention in 1963 with her article, "A Bunny's Tale," based on her time working at a Playboy Club. It was her 1969 article, "After Black Power, Women's Liberation," however, which really catapulted her into the limelight as a nationally-known figure.

In 1971, Steinem co-founded the National Women's Political Caucus, which was established to support women seeking political office. This was part of a broader effort to amplify women's voices within the political realm for more equitable representation.

Steinem was also a co-founder of the magazine Ms. in 1971. This publication was one of the first to address issues from a feminist perspective, covering a wide range of topics from politics and society to personal experiences. It provided a platform for feminist voices and became an influential medium in the women's liberation movement.
