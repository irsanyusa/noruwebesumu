The Voice has announced the coaching lineup for Season 26 of the show, and it includes new coaches Snoop Dogg and Michael Bublé. They’ll be mentoring next season’s talent alongside two returning coaches.

The Voice Announces Season 26 Coaches

On Monday, The Voice announced which celebrities will be sitting in the spinning red chairs for Season 26. Along with new coaches Snoop Dogg and Michael Bublé, Reba McEntire will be returning for her third consecutive season as coach. Meanwhile, on-and-off coach Gwen Stefani will be on for the new season.

Snoop and Bublé may be new to coaching, but they’re no strangers to The Voice. Snoop served as a Mega Mentor in Season 20, while Bublé was an advisor on Blake Shelton’s team all the way back in Season 3 of the show.

According to Deadline, Snoop called McEntire the “queen” of the show, adding, “I can be the king that brings the style, the funk, the fun.” Watching those two interact during the new season is sure to be fun. Season 26 of The Voice is expected to air this fall on NBC, with Carson Daly returning as host.

Season 25 Is Currently Airing

Season 25 of the show is currently airing on NBC. Live shows recently kicked off, as the competition narrows down. This season, the coaches are McEntire, John Legend, Chance the Rapper, and Dan + Shay (the show’s first coaching duo).

This is the show’s second season without veteran coach Blake Shelton, who left the coaching panel last year after 23 seasons on the show. Shelton was one of the show’s original coaches, and he didn’t miss a single season before he left.

Shelton met his wife Gwen Stefani when they both coached on the show. Season 26 will mark Stefani’s second season coaching without her husband. It will be her eighth season overall, having previously won Season 19 with artist Carter Rubin.
