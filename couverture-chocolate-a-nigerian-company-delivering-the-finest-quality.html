Couverture Chocolate vs. Compound Chocolate

The sad truth is that more often than not, the type of chocolate used to create desserts and baked goods is compound chocolate. Even the stuff you find on grocery shelves is normally compound chocolate. This commonality has nothing to do with quality, flavor, aroma or texture, however.

ADVERTISEMENT

Partly, this typical use of compound chocolate is attributed to many people not knowing that there is better quality available. The next factor is cost. Compound chocolate is more often cheaper to purchase compared to couverture chocolate.

Compound chocolate is so common and usually cheaper because it is easier to produce. This ease, and the subsequent drop in quality, comes from the use of cocoa powder instead of real cocoa solids.

Cocoa butter is also typically substituted with compound chocolate. Oils like coconut oil, vegetable oil and palm kernel oil are the usual substitutes. These properties mean that compound chocolate has a higher melting point in addition to it not requiring tempering. Chocolate tempering, which is the process of heating and cooling chocolate so that it stabilizes enough to make confections and candies. The process prevents the chocolate from melting easily when touched, gives the chocolate a smooth, glossy finish and allows a beautiful set when used for covering or dipping treats.

For the best taste and finished product, cheaper is not better. Instead, for authentic chocolate flavor, easier handling and a more aesthetically-pleasing end product, real quality chocolate AKA couverture chocolate should be used. Couverture chocolate is made using cocoa solids and cocoa butter. Cocoa butter percentage in couverture chocolate must be at least 30% to guarantee that delicious flavor. Some of Dùne chocolate contains as much as 70% cocoa.

The luxurious chocolate requires tempering for a smooth, glossy finish, crisp snap and hardness. The anatomy of couverture chocolate means that it is easier to handle and work with because it does not melt as easily as compound chocolate to the touch.

The superior quality of couverture chocolate comes from the quality of cocoa beans used. We at Dùne use the finest quality cocoa beans that are sourced locally in Nigeria. We also ensure the cocoa beans are stored using the best practices for a wholesome, fresh taste every time.

How couverture chocolate is manufactured also plays a major role in its superior quality. The smooth, creamy texture and deeper flavor are gained from the cocoa being ground much finer during production.
